You’ve brushed your teeth. You’ve fluffed your pillows. You’ve perfectly burrito’d yourself into your blanket cocoon, achieving peak coziness. And just as your soul begins to transcend into the realm of dreams… you see it. The ceiling light. Still on. Mocking you.


Lights Out (Eventually) captures that exact emotional collapse in ice cream form. A soothing honey vanilla base, infused with calming chamomile and faint notes of false security, is disrupted by swirls of “Regret Ripple”—a sticky, slow-moving caramel that represents the full-body sigh you let out when you realize you have to get up again. Hidden inside are chunks of crushed shortbread, representing your broken spirit, and a soft lavender aftertaste meant to console you while you shuffle back across the room like a betrayed ghost. Each bite delivers the warm, sleepy comfort of being in bed... followed by the cold, rude awakening of having to leave it because past-you didn’t plan ahead. Comes with a free tiny flashlight and a sense of existential exhaustion. Best enjoyed under covers, while angrily aiming the remote at a lamp that absolutely isn’t smart-enabled.
